On starting a commonplace book

Why keep a personal site in 2026, when every social network offers a bigger audience? Because the audience was never the point.

A commonplace book is written for its keeper. Into it go book reviews, film notes, plans and goals, technical articles, pages of mathematics — anything worth thinking about twice. Writing a thing down is how you find out whether you actually understand it.

So this site is deliberately small and deliberately old-fashioned:

  • Plain pages. No feed, no infinite scroll. You read to the end, and the page ends.
  • Plain files. Every entry is a text file on my machine. No database, no platform that can vanish and take the archive with it.
  • Plain manners. No tracking, no pop-ups asking you to subscribe.

If in ten years this page still opens in whatever browsers look like then — it will have succeeded.

Here’s to the old web. ❦
