12 Angry Men

Sidney Lumet, 1957

One room, twelve men, ninety-six minutes, and more tension than most thrillers manage with car chases. Lumet’s first feature is still the definitive proof that cinema needs nothing but faces and a script.

The craft is invisible but relentless: as the film goes on, Lumet lowers the camera and tightens the lenses, so the ceiling slowly presses down on the jurors. You feel the room getting smaller without ever noticing why.

And Fonda’s Juror 8 is not a hero because he knows the defendant is innocent — he doesn’t. He is a hero because he is willing to sit in the discomfort of not being sure. A civics lesson that never once feels like one.

Verdict: nine. Deducted one point only because everything afterwards feels slow for a day.
