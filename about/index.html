About this place

This is a commonplace book — an old English tradition: a private notebook where one copies passages from books, jots down thoughts, works through problems, and keeps whatever seems worth keeping. Scholars kept them from the seventeenth century onward. This one simply happens to be on the web.

Here you will find notes on books I’ve read, films I’ve watched, code and technical matters, a bit of mathematics, plans, goals, and miscellany — anything I care about enough to write down.

This site is deliberately old-fashioned. There are no algorithms deciding what you see, no tracking scripts watching you read, no comment section, no likes. Pages are plain HTML; they load instantly and will still work in twenty years. If you want to follow along, there is an RSS feed — the gentleman’s way of subscribing.
