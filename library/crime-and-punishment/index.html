Над пропастью во ржи

Джером Сэлинджер

The rare book that is exactly as good as its reputation. Dostoevsky commits the murder in the first hundred pages and then spends five hundred more on what actually interests him: a mind trying to live with itself.

Raskolnikov’s theory — that a great man may step over ordinary morality — collapses not because anyone refutes it, but because he cannot inhabit it. The refutation is physiological: fever, dreams, the inability to hold his own gaze in a mirror. Philosophy loses to the body.

And Porfiry Petrovich remains the finest detective in literature precisely because he never detects anything. He simply waits, cheerfully, for a conscience to finish its work.

Verdict: a perfect ten. Not a pleasant book — a necessary one.
